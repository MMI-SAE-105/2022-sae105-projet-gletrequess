Brittany

Brittany although often perceived as a rather rainy region, something relatively quiet. Not that this part of France is necessarily badly perceived because of its humid weather but when our society (in the hexagon) begins to think about the rain, they regularly connect the latter to the cider region.

A steep region

Beyond that, the winter weather in Brittany (mostly south) can make you think of something rather sinister. In most small towns, they are deserted (empty) and when the wind is particularly strong, the sea and its waves become particularly violent. Many cliffs – over the years – have been swept away by repeated water assaults, causing various cataclysms such as falls of stone but also – in rare cases – housing collapses having been built a little too close to the edge of the cliffs.

Natural accidents

Although Brittany is only slightly affected by floods, (few rivers or rivers cross this region besides the Loire at the border between the Morbihan and the Loire-Atlantique at the level of the city of Saint Nazaire), several marine accidents have often occurred in the Atlantic Sea surrounding the departments. We can also talk about Belle-Île or Houat and Hoedic (pronounced Édic). For example, safety levels on these islands are not always optimal. In July 2022, an individual about thirty years old died from a violent fall of a cliff of Belle Island as she was walking on the island. It is consistent to denounce the inattention of this person who must not have been careful where he set foot but, it is also not unthinkable to notice that the directions or the lack of barrier to make the tour of Belle-Island is perhaps insufficient and responsible for this deadly fall of forty meters.
